
ROAD MAP:

# Multiplayer Mission Progression Roadmap

## Phase 1 — Mission Roster and Player States

Create a server-authoritative mission roster when the level begins.

Track two separate rosters:

### Mission Roster

Records everyone who began or meaningfully participated in the mission.

Used for:

* Final survival percentage
* Personal rewards
* Death penalties
* Reconnect restoration
* End-of-level statistics

### Active Roster

Tracks players currently affecting level progression.

Used for:

* Waypoint requirements
* Extraction requirements
* Living-player checks
* Disconnect grace periods

Each player can have one mission state:

* Active
* Dead
* Waiting for waypoint respawn
* In extraction zone
* Extracted
* Disconnected during grace period
* Disconnected and inactive
* Reconnected

Store each player’s:

* Peer ID
* Persistent player identity
* Starting-roster status
* Alive or dead status
* Current personal waypoint
* Highest waypoint reached
* Extraction-zone status
* Extracted status
* Disconnect status
* Reconnect timer
* Death count
* Personal performance statistics

---

## Phase 2 — Individual Waypoint Progress

Each player records waypoint progress independently.

Example:

Player 1: Waypoint 4
Player 2: Waypoint 4
Player 3: Waypoint 3
Player 4: Dead

A player reaching a waypoint does not automatically advance the rest of the team.

A waypoint becomes secured when every currently living, connected, non-extracted player has reached it.

Extracted players do not block waypoint progression.

Dead players do not block waypoint progression.

Disconnected players continue blocking progression during their reconnect grace period.

After the grace period expires, they no longer block waypoint progression.

---

## Phase 3 — Secured Waypoint Events

When all required living players reach a waypoint:

* Mark the waypoint as secured
* Lock earlier sections of the level
* Open the next section
* Prevent unnecessary backtracking
* Update each eligible player’s respawn location
* Spawn waypoint rewards
* Allow dead players to be restored
* Save the team’s progress through the mission

Possible waypoint rewards:

* Health supplies
* Ammunition
* Armor
* Temporary team buffs
* Currency
* Bonus reward points
* Player respawns

---

## Phase 4 — Waypoint Reward Budget

Each secured waypoint grants a limited reward budget.

Example:

Waypoint reward budget: 4 points

Possible costs:

* Respawn one player: 2 points
* Health supply crate: 1 point
* Ammo supply crate: 1 point
* Armor supply: 1 point
* Temporary team buff: 2 points
* Save unused points for final rewards

More player deaths mean more waypoint rewards must be spent on respawns.

This creates a natural death penalty without permanently removing players from long missions.

Unused waypoint reward points can contribute to the final mission bonus.

---

## Phase 5 — Player Death and Spectating

When a player dies:

* Mark them as dead
* Disable their combat and movement controls
* Switch them into spectator mode
* Allow them to cycle through living teammates
* Remove them from current waypoint requirements
* Record their death for final scoring
* Wait until the next team-secured waypoint

Dead players do not automatically respawn.

When the next waypoint is secured, the team may trade part of its waypoint reward for a respawn.

A revived player:

* Respawns at the newly secured waypoint
* Returns with partial health
* Returns with limited ammunition
* Receives short spawn protection
* Loses temporary buffs
* Becomes part of future waypoint and extraction requirements again

---

## Phase 6 — Extraction Zone

Players reach the extraction zone independently.

Entering the zone does not automatically extract them.

A player inside the extraction zone is considered extraction-ready.

While extraction-ready, the player:

* Remains physically present in the level
* Can continue helping teammates
* Can leave the extraction zone
* Can still take damage
* Can still die
* Has not locked in their survival reward
* Can provide limited support from the safer area

The extraction zone should be safer than the rest of the level, but not completely safe.

Possible extraction-zone support:

* Covering approaching teammates
* Limited healing or resupply
* Defensive weapon stations
* Enemy marking
* Opening or closing nearby defenses
* Monitoring cameras or sensors
* Limited long-range support

---

## Phase 7 — Manual Extraction

A player must press the interaction button to extract.

Extraction should require a short uninterrupted interaction rather than happening instantly.

Once extracted:

* The character’s survival is locked in
* The player cannot return to the level
* The player cannot attack
* The player cannot heal or support teammates
* The player becomes a spectator
* The player counts as successfully extracted
* The player receives the personal extraction bonus
* The player no longer blocks waypoint or extraction requirements

This creates the choice:

Extract now and guarantee survival

or

Remain in the zone and continue helping the team at personal risk

---

## Phase 8 — Mission Completion Conditions

A mission can only succeed when:

* At least one player has manually extracted
* Every remaining connected living player has reached and resolved the extraction objective

Remaining living players must either:

* Extract
* Continue supporting until they choose to extract
* Die before mission resolution

The mission cannot complete with zero extracted players.

If every player dies before anyone extracts, the mission fails.

Extracted players remain locked in as survivors even if the remaining team later struggles.

---

## Phase 9 — Disconnect and Reconnect Handling

When a player disconnects:

* Keep them in the active roster temporarily
* Start a reconnect grace period
* Preserve their character and mission state
* Continue counting them for waypoint and extraction requirements during the grace period

This prevents players from disconnecting near the end of a level to make completion easier.

If the grace period expires:

* Remove them from active progression requirements
* Keep their mission record
* Do not allow their disconnect to improve the survival score
* Preserve their previous death, waypoint, and extraction information

If they reconnect during the mission:

* Restore their mission-roster status
* Restore their previous character state
* Restore their health and resources where appropriate
* Restore their personal waypoint
* Restore whether they were dead, active, extraction-ready, or extracted
* Add them back to active requirements when applicable

Already extracted players reconnect as spectators.

Dead players reconnect as spectators until the next eligible respawn.

---

## Phase 10 — Base Mission Rewards

Every eligible participant receives a base reward when the mission succeeds.

Possible base rewards:

* Experience
* Money
* Mission points
* Account progression
* Character progression
* Level unlocks
* Equipment unlock progress

Mission failure may provide reduced rewards depending on the game mode.

---

## Phase 11 — Team Survival Bonus

The team survival bonus is based on how many mission-roster players successfully extract alive.

Example:

4 players participated.

4 extracted alive: 100% extraction rate
3 extracted alive: 75% extraction rate
2 extracted alive: 50% extraction rate
1 extracted alive: 25% extraction rate

Disconnecting does not improve this percentage.

A player who dies and is later revived can still extract alive, but their death remains recorded for the no-death bonus.

Track both:

* Final extraction rate
* No-death completion rate

---

## Phase 12 — Waypoint Completion Bonus

Track whether each player reached each waypoint alive before it was secured.

Award a higher team bonus when more players survive through more checkpoints.

Possible factors:

* Percentage of waypoints reached alive
* Number of players alive at each secured waypoint
* Number of respawns purchased
* Number of reward points spent
* Number of reward points saved
* Whether every original player reached every waypoint
* Whether earlier areas were successfully secured

---

## Phase 13 — Personal Rewards and Penalties

Each player receives an individual result in addition to team rewards.

Possible personal bonuses:

* Reached extraction zone
* Successfully extracted
* Completed without dying
* Reached every waypoint alive
* Helped secure every waypoint
* Revived teammates
* Healed teammates
* Prevented damage
* Completed objectives
* Saved another player near death

Possible personal penalties:

* Deaths
* Failure to extract
* Excessive avoidable damage
* Friendly-fire penalties, if friendly fire exists
* Abandoning required objectives
* Spending the mission disconnected beyond the grace period

Personal rewards should remain smaller than the base and team rewards so the system stays cooperative.

---

## Phase 14 — Combat and Support Statistics

Track mission statistics for every player:

* Enemies defeated
* Damage dealt
* Boss damage dealt
* Assists
* Health restored
* Armor restored
* Damage prevented
* Defensive damage absorbed
* Teammates revived
* Objectives completed
* Waypoints reached alive
* Deaths
* Damage received
* Time spent supporting the extraction zone
* Successful extraction
* Supplies contributed or shared

Enemy defeat credit should include assists and meaningful participation rather than only final blows.

Raw damage taken should not provide a positive reward.

Defensive damage absorbed should count only when connected to a tank, shield, guard, or protection mechanic.

“Most Damage Taken” may still appear as a humorous statistic without giving bonus rewards.

---

## Phase 15 — End-of-Mission Commendations

Display performance commendations after the mission.

Possible commendations:

* Most Enemies Defeated
* Most Damage Dealt
* Most Health Restored
* Most Damage Prevented
* Most Teammates Revived
* Most Objectives Completed
* Most Assists
* Least Damage Taken
* Most Defensive Damage Absorbed
* Extraction Guardian
* Waypoint Vanguard
* Flawless Survivor
* Team Lifeline

Commendations provide small bonuses or cosmetic recognition.

They should not outweigh team success.

---

## Phase 16 — Final Reward Calculation

The final reward structure will combine:

Base Mission Reward

plus

Team Survival Bonus

plus

Waypoint Performance Bonus

plus

Personal Extraction Bonus

plus

Personal Performance Bonus

minus

Death Penalties

minus

Waypoint Rewards Spent on Respawns

A starting balance target:

* 50% base mission completion
* 25% team survival and waypoint performance
* 15% personal survival and extraction
* 10% personal commendations

This balance can be adjusted after playtesting.

---

## Phase 17 — Level Transition

After mission completion:

* Lock all remaining player controls
* Finalize mission statistics
* Calculate rewards
* Display the mission-results screen
* Save player and account progression
* Clear mission-specific roster data
* Reset waypoint state
* Reset extraction state
* Load the next level
* Spawn players at their appropriate starting locations
* Fade everyone back into gameplay

# Phase 18 — Tutorial Mission and Ship Headquarters

## Tutorial Mission: Base Under Attack

The first playable map will act as both the tutorial and the opening story mission.

The player begins inside a base that is under attack.

The tutorial should introduce mechanics naturally while the player attempts to escape.

Tutorial objectives may include:

* Basic movement
* Jumping and platforming
* Aiming and firing
* Reloading
* Weapon switching
* Taking damage and healing
* Reviving or assisting teammates
* Enemy combat
* Individual waypoint progression
* Team-secured waypoints
* Using supplies
* Interacting with doors, terminals, and objectives
* Entering the extraction zone
* Choosing whether to remain and support teammates
* Manually extracting

The base attack should escalate as the players progress.

Possible events:

* Emergency alarms
* Damaged lighting
* Locked or malfunctioning doors
* Enemy breaches
* Injured personnel
* Failing defenses
* Environmental hazards
* Optional rescue objectives
* Optional supply rooms
* A final defensive encounter near extraction

The mission ends when at least one player extracts and all remaining living players resolve the extraction objective.

---

## Tutorial Story Goal

The main objective is:

Learn the game systems and escape the base while it is under attack.

The tutorial should feel like an actual mission rather than a separate training room.

Players learn mechanics because they are necessary to survive and escape.

Optional tutorial performance can affect the initial rewards brought onto the ship.

Examples:

* Personnel rescued
* Supplies recovered
* Enemies defeated
* Optional areas secured
* All teammates extracted
* No player deaths
* Waypoint rewards preserved
* Special equipment recovered

---

## Ship Arrival

After escaping the tutorial base, the players arrive aboard a ship.

The ship becomes the team’s permanent:

* Home
* Headquarters
* Mission hub
* Upgrade area
* Equipment area
* Social space
* Story progression center

Future missions and levels will be launched from the ship.

After completing a mission, surviving players return to the ship.

Dead or failed characters return according to the game’s progression and respawn rules.

---

## Mission Loop

The primary game loop becomes:

Ship Headquarters

→ Prepare characters and equipment

→ Select a mission

→ Launch into the mission

→ Progress through individual and team waypoints

→ Complete objectives

→ Reach the extraction zone

→ Extract surviving players

→ Calculate mission rewards and statistics

→ Return to the ship

→ Purchase or unlock upgrades

→ Prepare for the next mission

---

## Ship Mission Selection

Future missions can be initiated from a central command area.

Possible mission-selection systems:

* Navigation console
* Mission table
* Holographic map
* Communications terminal
* Captain’s bridge
* Operations room

The mission interface may display:

* Mission name
* Location
* Primary objectives
* Optional objectives
* Enemy types
* Environmental hazards
* Recommended equipment
* Possible rewards
* Difficulty
* Survival modifiers
* Team size recommendations
* Previously completed performance

Only the server or host should confirm mission selection and launch.

Other players may vote, mark themselves ready, or suggest missions.

---

## Player Upgrade Area

Players purchase or obtain upgrades aboard the ship using points and resources earned from missions.

Possible upgrade resources:

* Experience
* Money
* Mission points
* Upgrade points
* Salvage
* Research materials
* Rare mission rewards
* Character-specific resources

Possible upgrade categories:

* Maximum health
* Movement abilities
* Weapon handling
* Reload speed
* Ammunition capacity
* Damage
* Armor
* Healing effectiveness
* Support abilities
* Class abilities
* Revive abilities
* Extraction support abilities
* Passive bonuses

Upgrades may be:

* Purchased
* Unlocked through progression
* Found during missions
* Earned from optional objectives
* Awarded through commendations
* Crafted from recovered materials

---

## Equipment and Loadout Area

The ship should include an area where players prepare before missions.

Possible functions:

* Select character or class
* Select weapons
* Equip armor
* Equip support items
* Assign consumables
* View weapon statistics
* Upgrade equipment
* Test equipment
* Save loadouts
* Compare upgrades

A firing range or training room could allow players to test new weapons and abilities before launching.

---

## Ship Expansion

The headquarters can grow as the team progresses.

Possible ship areas:

* Bridge
* Mission operations room
* Armory
* Medical bay
* Engineering
* Research laboratory
* Crew quarters
* Training room
* Firing range
* Cargo and salvage storage
* Hangar
* Communications room
* Trophy or commendation area

Mission rewards and story progress may unlock or improve these areas.

Examples:

* Upgraded medical bay improves respawn or recovery options
* Upgraded armory unlocks stronger weapons
* Upgraded research lab unlocks new abilities
* Upgraded engineering unlocks ship systems
* Upgraded operations room reveals more mission information

---

## Persistent Ship Progression

The ship provides visible long-term progression between missions.

Possible persistent progression:

* New rooms become accessible
* Damaged sections are repaired
* New crew members join
* Vendors become available
* Mission contacts appear
* Decorations and trophies are displayed
* Recovered equipment appears in storage
* Story events occur between missions
* New mission regions unlock

This allows mission success to improve both the players and their shared headquarters.

---

## Multiplayer Ship Behavior

The ship should be a synchronized multiplayer hub.

Players should be able to:

* Move around independently
* Change equipment
* Purchase personal upgrades
* Interact with stations
* View mission results
* Enter training areas
* Mark themselves ready
* Vote on missions
* Inspect other players’ loadouts

The host remains authoritative over:

* Mission selection
* Mission launch
* Shared ship upgrades
* Shared resources
* Story progression
* Major world-state changes

Personal upgrades and equipment remain tied to each player’s persistent profile.

---

## Initial Ship Arrival Sequence

After completing the tutorial mission:

* Fade out from the extraction sequence
* Display the tutorial results
* Award tutorial rewards
* Introduce the ship through a short arrival sequence
* Spawn players in the ship
* Introduce the mission-selection area
* Introduce the upgrade and equipment systems
* Allow players to explore
* Unlock the first set of post-tutorial missions

The ship arrival should mark the transition from the tutorial into the main game structure.
